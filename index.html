What is a Starter Site and Why Your Business Needs One

If you have been putting off getting your business online because you think you need a full website, a developer, or a big budget, this is for you.

Let me break down something called a starter site and why it might be exactly what your business needs right now.

First, what is a starter site?

A starter site is a small, professionally built website designed to give your business a credible online presence without the complexity or cost of a full website. Usually three pages. Home, About, and Contact. Clean design, mobile friendly, your own domain, your own business email. Nothing unnecessary. Everything that matters.

It is not a shortcut. It is a smart starting point.

Why does your business need to be online?

Think about how you find businesses today. You search. You check if they have a site. If they do not, you move on. Your customers are doing the exact same thing. Not being online does not mean you are invisible. It means you look unserious to people who were ready to pay you.

A starter site changes that. It tells the world your business is real, reachable, and professional. Without saying a word.

But I have Instagram and Facebook already

Social media is rented land. Tomorrow the algorithm changes, the platform goes down, your account gets flagged, and everything you built is gone. A website is yours. Your domain is yours. Nobody can take it down. It is the only piece of online real estate you actually own.

A starter site and social media work together. Social media brings attention. Your site converts that attention into trust.

What does a starter site actually include?

A proper starter site comes with a custom domain, your own address online like yourbusiness.co.ke. It comes with a mobile friendly design because most of your customers will find you on their phones. It comes with a WhatsApp contact button so customers can reach you in one tap. It comes with basic SEO setup so search engines can find you. And it comes with a professional business email like info@yourbusiness.co.ke so you stop sending quotes from a Gmail address.

That is your entire first step online. Built and live in 48 hours.

Who is a starter site for?

It is for the salon owner who keeps telling clients to find them on Instagram but knows a website would look more professional. It is for the freelancer sending proposals from a personal email address. It is for the small shop that wants to be found when someone searches their area. It is for anyone who knows they need to be online but has not found an affordable, simple way to do it.

If you are waiting until you have enough money for a full website, a starter site is what you do while you wait. It keeps you visible, it keeps you credible, and it costs a fraction of what agencies charge.

What happens when you outgrow it?

You will. That is the point. A starter site is a foundation, not a ceiling. When your business grows and you need bookings, a shop, or a full custom site, you already have a domain, an audience, and an established online presence to build on. You are not starting from scratch. You are upgrading.

The honest truth

Most small businesses do not need a KES 50,000 website right now. They need to be found. They need to look credible. They need a phone number and a WhatsApp button and a page that tells the world they are open for business.

That is a starter site. And it costs less than most people spend on airtime in a month.

Your business deserves to be found. Start there.
